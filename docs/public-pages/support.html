Common questions

Is my data safe?

Yes. Everything you enter is stored privately on your iPhone. Nothing is sent to a server, and there are no accounts, analytics, or third-party trackers. The full details are in our privacy policy.

I deleted the app and reinstalled it — where did my data go?

When iOS deletes an app, it removes the app's private storage with it. Because Debt Freedom v1.0 doesn't sync to the cloud, that means your plan is gone too. We're working on optional cloud sync as a future update so you can restore across devices. For now, treat the app as you would a paper notebook on your phone.

How do I reset everything and start over?

Open the app → Settings → scroll to Danger zone → Reset everything. This clears your plan, debts, goals, and ticked months, and sends you back to onboarding. There's no undo.

I found a bug — what should I include?

Email benoats@googlemail.com with:

  • Your iPhone model and iOS version (Settings → General → About).
  • The app version (Settings → About inside Debt Freedom).
  • What you were trying to do, what happened, and what you expected.
  • A screenshot if you can capture one.

The numbers look wrong / different from my bank statement

Debt Freedom is a planning tool. It projects what would happen if you stick exactly to the plan you entered. Real interest is calculated by your lender slightly differently (daily compounding, statement dates, fees, promo periods) so figures will drift from your statement over time. Always check your statement for the actual balance owed before making decisions, and update your debt balances in Settings as your real numbers change.

Can I get my money back?

Debt Freedom is free, so there's nothing to refund. If you've found something that makes the app unusable, please email us and we'll do our best to fix it.
